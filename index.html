Lizzie Smith

I wrote journal entries for 82 days, from March 11th when we began quarantine until June 1st when I was finally able to go back to work. I did these partly out of boredom, partly because I wanted to see how much would change in this span of time. Reflecting back on this time has been good for the soul. I learned so much about myself and was able to really stop and see the beauty of the world around me, something I did not do enough of in all the busyness. 2020 has been trying, but honestly, I would not change a thing. I hope this does not come across as shallow or offensive to anyone. I know that covid has brought hardship to so many lives and I realize others have been through much more pain than I have. I certainly do not want to overlook that. I hope you enjoy this window into my life.

3/17:
I woke up at 6 am to drive to Anderson with dad. We packed my whole dorm in three hours then drove back home on the same day to avoid having to get a hotel. It’s crazy to think I will probably never see this place again.

4/1:
I got accepted into VCU art school! My whole family was outside with me when I got the letter and I started crying. I have been so anxious about it since January. We had a big meal to celebrate and I got so many texts and calls from friends and family.

Back when there was a mask shortage.

Our quarantine garden. We planted tomatoes, beans, squash, cucumber, okra, lots of herbs, and sunflowers!

4/3:
I got up and made breakfast for the whole fam and then was so bored I actually did a full face of makeup and got dressed cute. I caught up on all of my school work for the week too. Then Carl came over and we enoned and read our books then biked our usual rounds through the neighborhood. We saw so many people today! I love living in the Northside and having so much intentional time to spend outside with friends. I’ve never lived in a neighborhood like this before or experienced such a beautiful spring.

4/9:
Went on a run again. Guess I’m a runner now, who would’ve thought? My legs hurt. Had an interior design progress check and a math test.

4/12:
Easter, socially distant picnic.

4/21:
started baking through bread recipes from the book Artisan Bread in 5 Minutes a Day. I am getting much better at making my regular sandwich loaves, but I also have made challah, chocolate bread, baguettes, beignets, and rosemary crescent rolls.

5/8:
Mom has been doing so well since her surgery in March. She is able to walk around and use her arms some, but she is still in pain when she tries to sleep at night. We think it will be safe enough for us to go back to work when she is done with her radiation treatments.

I learned to cut hair.

5/31:
This will conclude my quarantine journal, as I start work tomorrow and Carl is allowed back in the house. We are still being careful and I am not allowed to see many people, but I think it will feel a lot different now.

Before and after images of our Time Studio class, a glorious exercise in collaboration and communitiy building.

Putting together this website was more of a journey than I ever could have expected. The act of assembling everyone's work into this cohesive object was a beautiul thing. It was a very important reminder to me how lucky I am to have gotten to work with so many amazing artists this semester. To my Time Studio: you are amazing, I thank you endlessly.

A NOTE TO ALL VIEWERS: I would be happy to include your work in this collective documentation of the times. I want this to be a collaborative effort for the future, for us, and for everyone to remember. If you would like to include a piece of work, please email me at timestudiotimecapsule [at] gmail [dot] com and I'll try to throw your work up on here!
